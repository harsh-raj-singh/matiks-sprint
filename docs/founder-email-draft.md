Subject: Built a fast 60-second mental math sprint inspired by Matiks

Hi Sudhanshu, Mohan, and Sushant,

I have been following Matiks and the way you are turning mental math into a competitive daily habit. I built a small web prototype that explores a related single-player loop: a 60-second sprint where the player answers as many adaptive arithmetic questions as possible, gets instant scoring feedback, and submits one result to a Supabase-backed leaderboard after the round.

The prototype is intentionally narrow: no multiplayer, no copied Matiks branding, and no attempt to reproduce your product surface. The focus is latency and flow. Questions are generated locally in the browser, scoring happens instantly, and the backend is only touched after a run ends, so the actual playing experience stays smooth.

I would like to share it for feedback because it is in the same mental-math category and may be useful as a quick web experiment around low-latency sprint mechanics.

Link: https://matiks-sprint.vercel.app
